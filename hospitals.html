HHS Datasets about Hospitals

This is a list of HHS Datasets that contain information about hospitals.(Help improve this content by suggesting more datasets.)

Topic Why you might be interested Link to more info
Hospital costs Hospitals submit quarterly cost/expense reports to Medicare (CMS) according to Medicare's accounting rules. Cost categories include labor/wages, capital costs, uncompensated care/bad debt, medical education, bed occupancy Hospital 2552-10 Cost Report Data
Hospital payment adjusters Medicare establishes standard payments, then adjusts payments to hospitals depending on specific characteristics of each hospital. Examples of characteristics captured by this dataset include: cost of living (COLA), disproportionate share (DSH), sole community hospitals (SCH), small rural Medicare-dependent hospitals (MDHs). Learn more in the manual that describes payment processing here Provider specific file
Hospital characteristics Name, address, phone number, beds, staffing, ownership type, accreditation/certification, residency programs, medical school affiliation, Medicare/Medicaid participating provider. Service availability, direct/contracted service: laboratories, pharmacies, every department. Federally qualified health center (FQHC) indicator, CMS certification number, urban/rural indicator Provider of Services File OTHER Public spreadsheet with variables
Hospital quality Quality data for over 4,000 US hospitals. Indicators include satisfaction, readmissions, complications, death, timely and effective care, use of medical imaging. Hospital Compare, API, Linked data
Master charge rates Hospital level data for the top 100 most frequently billed Medicare discharges for more than 3,000 US hospitals. Includes both what is billed and what Medicare pays. Medicare Provider Charge Data: Inpatient, API
Hospital discharges The Healthcare Cost and Utilization Project (HCUP) databases contain the largest collection of longitudinal hospital all payer claims data. Representative samples of encounter-level information on inpatient stays (including pediatrics), emergency department visits and ambulatory care (e.g., outpatient surgery) are available. Hospital identifiers are frequently suppressed HCUP home, Example data elements

Forms


Find all approved forms that HHS uses to collect info from the public (individuals and companies). OMB approves these forms and provides information from the forms on reginfo.gov

For example, FDA OMB approved forms are listed here with OMB Control Numbers (i.e., ID), user-generated tags, and abstracts. Refer back to reginfo.gov for more info using the OMB Control Number.

On reginfo.gov, go to the Information Collection Review (ICR) > Search page here

  1. Select Agency: Department of Health and Human Services
  2. Select Sub-Agency: ___________
  3. Select ICR Status: Active
  4. Click: Search

    5. Notes:

    For HHS forms, it is necessary to select a "Sub-Agency" because reginfo allows at most 1,000 results.

    "Sub-Agencies" do not necessarily correspond with HHS operating and staff divisions. Rather the options best correspond with budgeting allocations.

    So what?

    • Ever wondered what goes into an application to get a drug approved and marketed for sale in the United States? Find out! Make sure all the checkboxes are selected. See form. Or just download.
    • What is the legal basis for FDA's authority to approve drugs? Read "Authorizing Statute(s)" that are always cited here. For example: Authorizing Statute(s): US Code: 42 USC 262 Name of Law: PHS Act US Code: 21 USC 356b Name of Law: FFDCA
    • How many adverse event report responses are anticipated? Read the section 12 to find out how burden is calculated.
